The Weaknesses (Areas for Iteration):

* Visual Hierarchy in Settings: The settings menu is powerful but visually dense. It feels a bit like a stock Android system menu. It could benefit from better iconography, distinct section cards, and perhaps a "Basic" vs. "Advanced" toggle to avoid overwhelming casual users.
* Micro-interactions: Some menus and pop-ups lack the fluid, physics-based animations (like spring animations) that make top-tier apps feel "premium."
* Implement Material You: If it doesn't already, ensure the app pulls colors from the user's system wallpaper to make the browser feel like a native extension of their OS.
* Speed Benchmarking: Continuously profile the app against Brave and Firefox Mobile. If Easy Browser is lighter on battery and RAM, publish those metrics in the app description.
